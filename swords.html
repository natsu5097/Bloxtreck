Swords Overview

Swords are a weapon classification of up-close-and-personal melee weapons that can be found and bought everywhere in Blox Fruits. Most swords have a focus on precise, close-ranged moves that deal high damage. All swords have two unique abilities, and some swords have imbued or skills that originate from their Blox Fruit counterpart (or from somewhere else).

All swords can also be upgraded via the Blacksmith and enchanted via the Dragon Talon Sage to deal extra damage or to gain unique abilities relative to the enchantments.

Obtainment

There are several ways of obtaining swords, including:

  • Buying swords from NPCs.
  • Drops from bosses.
  • Completing puzzles and tasks, although these are usually boss drops as well.
  • In the case of Dark Blade, players can obtain the Game Pass by purchasing it with Robux R$ , receiving it as a gift, or trading for it.

Notes

  • All swords and their abilities cannot harm Blade users due to their passive immunity to swords, even by using Aura. However, certain sword abilities will still be able to stun-lock Blade users.
  • However, bosses with swords can still damage Blade users because bosses can overcome all immunities.
  • This also applies to the Chef who uses Blade, although he has a rare (1/3) chance of being hit.
  • Koko is the only sword which is buffed by a fruit other than Buddha, and that fruit is Control.
  • When the user is in Control Area, Koko's range and AoE output are significantly increased.
  • In their transformations, Buddha can increase the size of the sword and the AoE of its M1 output of fighting styles or swords.
  • Some sword abilities may not be affected by transformation. (e.g. Midnight Blade)
  • The [M1] attacks' damage is slightly reduced by Buddha's transformation, but due to the defense buff of Buddha and increased AoE, this is not a significant issue.
  • When Aura is active while using certain higher rarity swords, they will emanate a glowing aura, as seen with the Cursed Dual Katana.
  • Many swords have Destructible Physics, similar to many other Guns, Fruits, and fighting styles.
  • Some swords have level prerequisites (e.g. Tushita requires the player to be Lv. 2000+)
  • Swords at higher rarities, especially Mythical and Legendary tend to have higher raw damage than fruits (both M1's and skills), presumably to make up for their fewer skills and inferior mobility.
  • If the player is playing on console, swords will appear in their hotbar by name.
