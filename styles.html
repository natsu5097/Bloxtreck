Fighting Styles Overview

Fighting styles are an essential part in Blox Fruits. They are the core mechanism of the combat system in the game, and they can be obtained from the different teachers of a specific fighting style.

Compared to other weapon types, fighting styles involve mostly physical strikes, with less emphasis on weapons or super-powered abilities. Most have three combat moves, while Blox Fruits have 4-5 on average, and guns/swords have only two. Fighting styles tend to focus more on versatility and utility when compared to other weapon types, although some are capable of outputting heavy raw damage.

Most fighting styles focus on utility more than precise damage and have three moves, which allows them to be more versatile than the other weapon types.

Players who first join the game will receive Combat automatically. Upon learning a new fighting style, Combat will be replaced and cannot be relearned.

Notes

  • If the player had previously obtained a fighting style but switched to another one, relearning the original one will be free.
  • To obtain all fighting styles, it would cost a total of $25,400,000 and ƒ31,500.
  • All fighting styles will keep their mastery even when the player learns a new one.
  • Using a Fighting Style on enemies or players that have Elemental immunity will not damage them.
  • Using Aura bypasses their intangibility.
  • Most fighting styles have less range than Swords.
  • Combat is the only Fighting Style that does not have the Destructible Physics.
  • Instinct Breaking moves can be found here.

Trivia

  • All fighting styles have three different moves, except Combat (two), and Death Step/Dark Step (four).
  • Extra moves on four-skill styles mainly boost damage.
  • Dragon Breath is the only fragment-only style.
  • Sanguine Art and Combat have no "V2".
  • Godhuman and Sanguine Art require Materials.
  • All First Sea teachers are Shop NPCs, while Second and Third Sea teachers are Misc. NPCs.
